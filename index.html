About

I am an aspiring Digital Historian with a particular interest in the medieval period. Some of my research interests include manuscript transmission, Irish monasticism, and papal manuscripts. I earned a Bachelor of Arts in History from the University of Nebraska at Omaha in May 2015; I simultaneously earned a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology Innovation from the same institution. I intend to utilize my interdisciplinary skillset to produce, maintain, and manage Digital Humanities projects.

I have been a web developer since May 2012, and over the last four years have also taken on the role of project manager and “Scrum Master” within my development teams. I have experience working with modern web frameworks, such as Laravel and Ruby on Rails; databases, including MySQL and Postgres; and both new and established web trends, such as HTML5 and Sass.

When I’m not working on academic research or building websites, I enjoy weekly trivia, live music, and quiet nights in with my cat, Scout.
