About

I am a digital humanist, public historian, and web developer. My historical research focus is medieval medical manuscripts and changes in the medical field after the Black Death. I focus my work on public-oriented outcomes, and am specifically concerned with making academic research applicable and available to wider audiences. To this end, I leverage my background as a web developer to look for new ways to reach audiences and engage with historical research.

I have been a web developer since 2012, with a short break to complete my MA in history, and also took on the role of project manager and “Scrum Master” within my development teams. I have experience working with modern web frameworks, such as Laravel and Ruby on Rails; databases, including MySQL and Postgres; and both new and established web trends, such as HTML5 and Sass. I'm interested in questions of accessibility as it relates to web development as a whole and to digital humanities projects specifically.

Outside of my work, I enjoy vinyl records, high fantasy novels, midcentury-modern furniture, and calligraphy projects on the side. When I have time, I like to show off my pop culture knowledge at trivia events, and bird-watch with my cat, Scout.
